## Content Structure — Point by Point

### 1. The Opening Hook (200 words)
- Open with a real patient scenario (anonymised): patient compliant for 2 years, 3 months of neglect, disease recurred
- The emotional punch: "It wasn't her fault. It was a systems failure."
- Stat: 50% of adults have some form of gum disease (EFP data)
- Position: this post is about the gap nobody talks about

### 2. The Clinical Reality (400 words)
- Periodontal disease is chronic — it requires daily management, not just periodic treatment
- The 8,760 hours between annual visits vs the 2 hours in the chair
- What actually happens biologically when daily care drops (biofilm, pocket depth increase, bone resorption timeline)
- Dr. Anca's clinical perspective: "I can do perfect scaling and root planing, but if the patient doesn't maintain at home, I'll see the same pockets in 3 months"

### 3. Why Traditional Patient Education Fails (400 words)
- Ebbinghaus forgetting curve applied to dental instructions (40-80% forgotten within 24 hours)
- The 3 structural failures:
  1. **Information overload** in the chair
  2. **No feedback loop** at home
  3. **No accountability** between visits
- Compare to diabetes management: patients get glucose monitors, apps, daily check-ins. Periodontal patients get a leaflet.

### 4. What Actually Works — The Evidence (400 words)
- WHO 2023: consistent daily routines reduce periodontal disease progression by 60-70%
- Spaced repetition and habit formation research (Lally et al., 2009: average 66 days to form a habit)
- The power of reminders: studies showing smartphone notifications improve health behaviour compliance by 25-40%
- Accountability mechanisms: patients who know someone checks on them perform better

### 5. The Bridge Between Chair and Home (300 words)
- This is where Perioskoup sits — not replacing the dentist, extending the reach
- Brief, natural mention: "This is exactly why we built Perioskoup"
- The clinician stays in control, AI personalises delivery and timing
- Not a product pitch — a clinical argument for why technology is the missing layer

### 6. What Patients Can Do Right Now (300 words)
- 3 actionable steps any patient can take today (no app needed):
  1. Ask your periodontist to write down your 3 most important home care instructions
  2. Set a daily phone alarm for your interdental cleaning
  3. Track your habits for 2 weeks — even a paper calendar works
- Transition: technology makes this easier and more personalised, but the principle is consistency

### 7. What Clinicians Can Do Right Now (200 words)
- Limit instructions to 3 per visit
- Use teach-back method
- Follow up within 48 hours (even a text message)
- Consider digital tools that extend your reach

### 8. Closing — The Vision (150 words)
- Periodontal disease is manageable. The gap between appointments is fixable.
- The future of dental care is not more appointments — it's better support between them.
- Dr. Anca's personal sign-off: her clinical commitment to bridging this gap

## FAQ Schema (JSON-LD — include at bottom of post)

1. **Q: What happens to your gums between dental visits?**
   A: Without consistent daily care, bacterial plaque reforms within 24-48 hours after professional cleaning. Over weeks and months, this can lead to gum inflammation, increased pocket depths, and bone loss — even in patients who have received excellent clinical treatment.

2. **Q: How can I prevent periodontal disease from coming back?**
   A: Consistent daily interdental cleaning, twice-daily brushing with fluoride toothpaste, and regular professional maintenance every 3-4 months. The key factor is consistency — research shows patients who maintain daily habits reduce disease progression by 60-70%.

3. **Q: How often should periodontal patients see their dentist?**
   A: Patients with a history of periodontal disease typically need professional maintenance every 3-4 months, depending on disease severity and individual risk factors. Your periodontist will set the appropriate interval based on your clinical examination.

4. **Q: Can technology help with dental care between appointments?**
   A: Yes. Digital tools that provide personalised reminders, habit tracking, and daily guidance have been shown to improve patient compliance with home care routines. They extend the reach of clinical recommendations into the patient's daily life.
