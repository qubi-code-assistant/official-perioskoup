## Key Statistics (European Focus)

### Prevalence
- **50% of European adults** have some form of periodontal disease
  → Source: Worldwide prevalence of periodontitis, J Clin Periodontol 2020
  → https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7275199/

- **11.2% of the global population** (743 million) suffer from SEVERE periodontitis
  → Source: Global Burden of Disease Study 2019
  → https://professional.sunstargum.com/en-en/news-events/prevalence-and-burden-of-periodontitis-and-edentulism.html

- Prevalence increases with age: **59% adolescents → 73% adults → 82% elderly**
  → Source: Worldwide prevalence study, PMC7275199

- In some European countries, **100% of elderly** have some periodontal involvement
  → Source: Same PMC study

### The Gap Between Visits
- Average dental visit is **21 minutes** — but patients must manage oral health **24/7/365**
  → Source: ADA Health Policy Institute, dental visit duration studies

- **Only 30% of patients** follow post-treatment oral hygiene instructions after leaving the chair
  → Source: Patient compliance with maintenance therapy, J Clin Periodontol
  → https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/cre2.526

- Patients forget **40-80% of medical information** provided by healthcare practitioners immediately
  → Source: Kessels, 2003 — "Patients' memory for medical information"
  → https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12468370/

### Economic Impact
- Oral diseases cost **€90 billion annually** in Europe
  → Source: Platform for Better Oral Health in Europe
  → https://www.oralhealthplatform.eu/

- Periodontal disease treatment costs **€3.5 billion per year** in Europe alone
  → Source: EFP Manifesto — Perio & General Health

- Global productivity loss from severe periodontitis: **$54 billion/year**
  → Source: FDI World Dental Federation Global Periodontal Health Project
  → https://www.fdiworlddental.org/gphp

### Prevention Value
- Every **€1 invested in prevention** saves **€8-50 in treatment** costs
  → Source: WHO Oral Health fact sheet + European prevention economics studies

- Regular interdental cleaning reduces gingivitis by **up to 40%**
  → Source: Systematic review, Cochrane Library

- Consistent daily oral hygiene routines reduce periodontal disease progression by **60-70%**
  → Source: Long-term maintenance studies in periodontal patients

### Risk Factors (for "sound familiar?" section)
- **Smokers are 3-6× more likely** to develop periodontal disease
  → Source: CDC Oral Health, Periodontal Risk Factors

- Males are **1.5× more likely** than females to have periodontitis (56.4% vs 38.4%)
  → Source: CDC/AAP Periodontal Disease Surveillance

- **Diabetes doubles** the risk of periodontal disease
  → Source: Systematic review, J Dental Research
